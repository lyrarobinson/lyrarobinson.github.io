Alternative Operations uses real-time action classification to display a flood of training data taken from Moments in Time, corresponding to a viewer's action/inaction.

Moments in Time is a collection of one million annotated 3 second videos, designed to correspond to the average duration of human short-term memory.

Construction of the dataset was supervised by research scientists at MiT. Videos were primarily scraped from youtube, and categorised in several rounds by Amazon Mechanical Turk workers.

The categories used in the final set are intentionally vague - 'opening' could refer to a door, a gate, or an eye.

Overworked and underpaid Mechanical Turkers are required to decide whether a certain video fits into a vague category - categories that cross linguistic and cultural barriers.

This taxonomy often fails, and in extreme cases, as Everest Pipkin pointed out, the categories 'kissing' and 'hitting' document unconsensual acts and excessive violence*.

As well as a linguistic issue, the dataset also presents a technological one. The classifications in this piece are made by 2d resnet model, released by MiT to other researchers and pre-trained on the Moments in Time set. As you will notice - it is often inaccurate.

The model will regularly default to categories with a higher internal visual consistency, and always seeks to recognise an action even when no action is performed.

*These categrories have been removed from this installation.
